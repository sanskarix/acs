What Is a Home Loan?

A home loan – also called a housing loan or mortgage – is a secured, long-tenure loan used to purchase a ready-to-move property, buy a plot and construct a house, or fund the renovation of an existing home. The property you are buying or constructing serves as collateral for the loan.

Because the loan is secured, home loans carry significantly lower interest rates than unsecured products such as personal loans. Tenures can extend over several decades, which keeps monthly EMIs manageable even on large loan amounts.

Who Should Consider a Home Loan?

Home loans are suitable for:

  • First-time homebuyers purchasing a flat or house
  • Individuals buying a plot and planning to construct a residential property
  • Existing homeowners who want to renovate or extend their property
  • Borrowers with an existing loan who want to transfer their balance to a lower-rate lender
  • NRIs looking to purchase property in India

Already own property? If you need funds for business or personal use beyond home purchase, a secure loan against property lets you borrow against your existing real estate at competitive rates. Our advisors can help you weigh both options.

Key Advantages of a Home Loan

  • Lower interest rates compared to unsecured loans
  • Long repayment tenures keep monthly EMIs affordable relative to the loan amount
  • Tax benefits on both principal repayment and interest paid, subject to statutory limits
  • Financing options designed to minimize your upfront down payment burden
  • Option to choose between fixed and floating interest rates based on your risk preference
  • Possibility of adding a co-applicant to increase eligible loan limits
  • Balance transfer option available if you find a lender offering a lower rate
  • Prepayment options with minimal or zero penalties for individual floating-rate borrowers

Home Loan Eligibility – What Do Lenders Assess?

Home loan eligibility is determined by a combination of your income, credit history, property characteristics, and the lender's internal policies. Actual criteria vary by lender and city.

Employment Type Salaried (private/public/MNC) or self-employed with stable business income history
Minimum Monthly Income Varies by city, lender policies, and overall loan size
Age Limits Repayment must typically conclude before retirement or a defined age limit
Credit Score (CIBIL) Healthy score preferred; higher scores qualify for the most competitive rates
Debt-to-Income Ratio Total monthly obligations must fall within standard lender ratios
Property Eligibility Property must be legally clear, approved by local authorities, and valued by the lender's empanelled experts

Note: Adding a co-applicant with a regular income can significantly increase your eligible loan amount. Eligibility criteria differ across lenders – a profile that doesn't qualify with one bank may be approved by another. We assess your profile first before any formal application.

Why Do Home Loan Applications Get Rejected?

Due to the high loan amounts involved, home loan underwriting is thorough. Key reasons for application rejection include:

  • Low Credit Score: A poor CIBIL report due to missed payments or defaults is a primary cause of rejection.
  • High Debt Obligation: If your current income is heavily committed to credit cards or existing personal/car loans, lenders may decide you cannot support a new housing EMI. Compare this with car refinance loans to see how lowering other EMIs can help.
  • Property Legal Issues: If the property lacks clear title deeds, local municipal approvals, or has pending legal disputes, lenders will decline to finance it.
  • Inadequate Income Proof: Unverifiable income or cash salary without bank records makes it difficult for lenders to assess repayment capacity.
  • Unstable Employment History: Frequent job changes within short periods can signal job instability to risk-averse lenders.

Common Mistakes to Avoid When Applying for a Home Loan

Avoid these critical mistakes to secure a smoother approval and better long-term financing:

  • Applying to Multiple Banks Simultaneously: Every application triggers a hard inquiry, which drops your credit score. Work with an advisory to apply strategically.
  • Overlooking Processing and Legal Fees: Interest rates are not the only cost. High processing fees, valuation charges, and administrative expenses can add up significantly.
  • Opting for the Longest Tenure Unnecessarily: Selecting a multi-decade tenure lowers your monthly payment but increases the cumulative interest paid. Use our EMI Calculator to compare different tenures.
  • Neglecting Prepayment Terms: While floating-rate loans have zero prepayment charges for individuals, terms can differ for business entities or fixed-rate structures. Check these terms in advance.

How the Home Loan Process Works – Step by Step

A home loan involves more stages than an unsecured loan, primarily because of property verification. Here is a realistic overview of the steps involved.

  1. 1
    Consultation & Pre-Qualification Share your income, existing obligations, and target property details. We assess your eligibility, estimate how much you can borrow, and use our EMI calculator to analyze monthly cash flows.
  2. 2
    Lender Shortlisting & Offer Comparison We identify lenders best suited to your profile – considering interest rates, processing fees, prepayment terms, and turnaround times.
  3. 3
    Document Compilation & File Submission We review and organize your income and property documents, then submit a complete application to the chosen lender to minimize queries.
  4. 4
    Credit Appraisal & Technical Verification The lender assesses your creditworthiness and assigns empanelled legal and technical experts to evaluate the property's title and value.
  5. 5
    Sanction Letter Issuance On successful appraisal, the lender issues a sanction letter specifying the approved amount, rate, tenure, and conditions. We review the terms with you.
  6. 6
    Agreement Signing & Loan Disbursal After signing the loan agreement and paying processing fees, funds are disbursed – in one tranche for ready property or in stages for under-construction purchases.

Frequently Asked Questions – Home Loans

  • A home loan is a secured loan used to purchase, construct, or renovate a residential property. The property serves as collateral. You repay through monthly EMIs over a chosen tenure. The lender holds a charge on the property until full repayment.

  • Home loan interest rates in India vary depending on the lender, your credit profile, loan amount, and tenure. Salaried borrowers with strong CIBIL scores usually qualify for the most competitive rates. Floating rates change with benchmark rate fluctuations, which may rise or fall over the tenure.

  • Most banks and housing finance companies offer tenures spanning multiple decades, subject to the borrower's age at maturity. A longer tenure lowers the EMI but increases the total interest paid. Use our EMI calculator to compare different tenure scenarios.

  • Lenders allow a home loan EMI up to a percentage of your net monthly income. You may be eligible for a loan amount that is a multiple of your net annual salary, subject to existing debt obligations. Adding a co-applicant can increase your eligibility significantly.

  • Lenders can finance a substantial percentage of the property value depending on regulatory guidelines and loan size. The borrower is required to contribute the remainder as down payment. Government taxes, stamp duty, and registration charges are typically not covered by the loan.

  • A fixed-rate loan locks your interest rate for a set period, providing EMI certainty. A floating rate is linked to a benchmark and changes as the benchmark changes. Most home loans in India are issued at floating rates. The choice depends on whether you value predictability or potential savings in a declining rate environment.
