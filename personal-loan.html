What Is a Personal Loan?

A personal loan is an unsecured credit product – no property or asset needs to be pledged as collateral. You receive a fixed sum, which you repay through equated monthly instalments (EMIs) over a tenure you agree upon with the lender, typically ranging from one to five years.

Because there is no security involved, lenders assess the risk based on your credit score, income stability, employer reputation, and existing debt obligations. This means the rate you receive can vary significantly from one lender to another.

Who Should Consider a Personal Loan?

Personal loans work well for salaried employees and self-employed individuals who need immediate funds without pledging an asset. Common use cases include:

  • Medical emergencies or elective treatment
  • Home renovation or repair not covered by a home loan (Read our guide on Home Loans)
  • Wedding or family function expenses
  • Consolidating multiple high-interest debts into a single EMI
  • Funding a short-term education course or certification
  • Travel or relocation costs

Note: If you own property and need a larger loan amount at a lower rate, a secure loan against property may be more cost-effective. Our advisors can help you compare both options for your specific situation.

Practical Advantages of a Personal Loan

  • No collateral required – your assets remain unencumbered
  • Disbursal can happen within a few working days with complete documentation
  • Competitive loan amounts scaled to your income and lender policies
  • Flexible repayment tenures let you manage monthly cash flow
  • Fixed EMI throughout the loan tenure makes budgeting predictable
  • No restriction on end use – spend it on whatever personal need you have
  • Good repayment history improves your credit score over time

Personal Loan Eligibility – What Do Lenders Look For?

Every lender sets their own criteria. The table below reflects typical benchmarks across most banks and NBFCs. Exact requirements will vary.

Employment Type Salaried (private/public sector/MNC) or self-employed with established business vintage
Minimum Monthly Income Varies by city, employer category, and lender policies
Age Limits Minimum age of majority up to retirement age (varies by lender)
Credit Score (CIBIL) Healthy credit score preferred; higher scores help secure the most competitive rates
Work Experience Minimum period with current employer and stable total experience
Debt-to-Income Ratio Existing obligations must fall within standard lender limits

Important: Eligibility criteria differ by lender. A profile that is declined by one bank may be approved by another at a competitive rate. We evaluate your profile first to identify suitable lenders before submitting any application.

Why Do Personal Loan Applications Get Rejected?

Understanding the common reasons for rejection can help you strengthen your application before submitting it to a lender. Key factors include:

  • Low Credit Score: A CIBIL score below the lender's threshold indicates past credit risk and is the most common reason for auto-rejection.
  • High Existing Debt: If a large portion of your monthly income is already going towards current EMIs, lenders may decide you do not have the capacity to repay a new loan. Compare this with a secured loan against property, which might offer more flexible debt ratios.
  • Incomplete or Unverified Documents: Mismatches in your PAN, Aadhaar, salary slips, or bank statements can cause immediate rejection during verification.
  • Inadequate or Unstable Income: Lenders require proof of a stable monthly income to ensure you can support repayments.
  • Frequent Job Changes: Rapidly changing employers indicates low stability, which traditional banks view as a risk factor.

Common Mistakes to Avoid When Applying for a Personal Loan

To secure the most competitive terms and protect your financial health, avoid these common pitfalls:

  • Applying to Too Many Lenders at Once: Each application triggers a hard inquiry on your credit report. Multiple inquiries in a short period signal credit hunger and can lower your credit score.
  • Ignoring Processing Fees and Hidden Charges: A low headline interest rate can be offset by high processing fees, documentation charges, or annual administrative costs. Always compare the overall cost.
  • Choosing the Longest Tenure Without Comparing Total Interest: While a longer tenure reduces your monthly EMI, it significantly increases the total interest you will pay over the life of the loan. Use our EMI Calculator to find the optimal balance.
  • Not Checking Prepayment and Foreclosure Charges: If you plan to pay off your loan early, high prepayment penalties can make foreclosure expensive. Check these clauses before signing the agreement.

Documents Required for a Personal Loan

Having your documents in order significantly speeds up processing. Below is a typical checklist – your lender may ask for additional items based on your profile.

Identity & Address
  • PAN Card
  • Aadhaar Card
  • Passport / Voter ID / Driving Licence (any one as address proof)
Income – Salaried
  • Last 3 months' salary slips
  • Last 6 months' bank statement (salary account)
  • Form 16 or IT Returns (latest year)
  • Employment letter or offer letter
Income – Self-Employed
  • IT Returns for last 2–3 years
  • Profit & Loss statement and Balance Sheet
  • Last 12 months' business bank statements
  • GST registration (if applicable)
Additional
  • Passport-size photograph
  • Loan application form (lender-specific)
  • Existing loan statements (if applicable)
